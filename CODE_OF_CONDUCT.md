# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a friendly, safe, and welcoming environment for everyone, regardless of experience level, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, appearance, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or nationality.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Use of sexualized language or imagery
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission

### ABA Industry-Specific Terms

As a project serving the special education field, we additionally require:

1. **Person-First Language**: Use "child with autism" rather than "autistic child", respecting the dignity of individuals served
2. **Data Protection**: Sharing identifiable clinical data in any public channel is prohibited, including but not limited to combinations of names, institution names, and specific behavior descriptions
3. **Professional Ethics**: Follow BACB ethical guidelines regarding data protection and client rights
4. **Respect for Families**: Avoid value judgments about family parenting approaches in discussions

Instances of Code of Conduct violations may be reported to [contact to be configured]. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated, and will result in a response deemed necessary and appropriate.
